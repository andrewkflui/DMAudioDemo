Low Pass Filter and Aliasing Demo

Developed by Dr. Andrew Kwok-Fai LUI

You should now see an image scaled-up from a small version. The "blockiness" is due to the nearest-neighbour scaling up operation that simply copies a pixel's value to the gap after enlarge.

The "blockiness" is an effect called aliasing. From the perspective of "rate of change", the "blockiness" is a high frequency (fast rate of change) signal.

Human eyes are sensitive to lines and so the "blockiness" is the most prominent feature seen by you.

A low-pass filter can filter out some of the high frequency signal.

Pressing the button will perform a low-pass filter on the image and the blockiness will be removed.
