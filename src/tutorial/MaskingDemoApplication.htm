Frequency Masking Demo

Developed by Dr. Andrew Kwok-Fai LUI

Learning Objective: Describe the effect of frequency masking.

NOTE: Some students may not be able to experience the masking effect because of the sensitivity of their ears. Please ignore this activity.

The interface provides controls to two waveform generator. We will use Generator A to output the main audio signal, and Generator B to output a weaker signal that is going to be masked.

Set Generator A to frequency of around 1000 Hz and the Amplitude to 50%.

Set Generator B to frequency of around 200 Hz and the Amplitude to 5%. You should be able to barely hear the lower frequency audio signal.

Gradually increase the frequency of Generator B to around 800 Hz. You should still be able to hear that there are two frequencies. Increase the frequency of Generator B further and suddenly there should be a point that you cannot hear the lower frequency audio signal. Note that frequency down. This frequency should be around 820 Hz, but it could be different in your case.

Now set Generator B to 1000 Hz. Increase the frequency gradually until you can hear a higher frequency sound. Again, note the frequency down.

You have now written down two frequencies. The range of frequency between the two is the region that is masked by the 1000 Hz audio signal.
